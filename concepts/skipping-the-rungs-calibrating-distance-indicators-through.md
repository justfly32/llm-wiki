# Skipping the rungs! Calibrating distance indicators through their clustering with galaxies

> 📄 원문: [Skipping the rungs! Calibrating distance indicators through their clustering with galaxies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06567v1)
> ✍️ 저자: Madeline L. Cross-Parkin, Leonardo Giani, Cullan Howlett
> 📅 발행: 2026-07-07
> 🏷️ 카테고리: astro-ph.CO

## 요약

We show that angular cross-correlations between distance indicators and galaxy redshift catalogues, when interpreted within an assumed cosmological model, can constrain potential biases in distance measurements induced by calibration systematics. As a test case, we consider a simple scenario in which a constant calibration offset $ΔM$ shifts all observed distances by a multiplicative factor, and we produce Fisher forecasts for the constraining power on $ΔM$ and $H_0$ from existing and upcoming surveys. In our most optimistic scenario, based on the expected number of SN Ia observed by LSST and the DESI final data release, we find $σ_{ΔM} \approx 0.05$ and $σ_{H_0} \approx 1.81~\mathrm{km~s^{-1}~Mpc^{-1}}$. This has important implications for the Hubble tension, since explaining the discrepancy purely as a calibration systematic in low-$z$ measurements would require $ΔM \approx 0.13$.
